Because we will not have regular face-to-face class sessions, it is very important that you find ways to stay internally disciplined, organized, and motivated. Here are some tips to be successful:

  • Check the course daily, to stay in touch with the material and activities and keep them fresh in your mind.
  • Read all announcements! This is the only way your instructor can update you on any new course information. Announcements will be sent to your ASU email address. (The one that ends with @asu.edu.) If you do not check this email regularly, you will need to be sure to start. If you have set your Canvas settings not to receive announcements by email, then you need to be sure to check the announcements within the course. You are responsible for knowing the content of the announcements posted in the course.
  • Create a personal organization system, such as a calendar, a to-do list, smartphone reminders, or some other organizational tool, to keep track of due dates specified. (Canvas's calendar may be able to help, if you like this format.)
  • Communicate regularly with your instructor and peers.
  • Create a study and/or assignment schedule to stay on track. This is an online course, and the pace goes very quickly. For a 3-credit course, the expectation is that students will spend about 135 hours on the material through the semester. For various course lengths, this breaks down as follows:
    Course Length Approximate # of hours
    required per week
    Summer A or B (6 weeks) ~23
    Summer C (8 weeks) ~17
    Fall/Spring A or B (7.5 weeks) ~18
    Fall/Spring C (16 weeks) ~9

Of course, the actual amount of time you need may vary based on a lot of factors, but it would be wise to clear your schedule of the appropriate number of hours per week, in order to give yourself the best chance of doing well in this course.

  • Set aside regular times in which you can do your classwork with minimal interruptions or distractions. Avoid multi-tasking while doing online studying. Treat this with the same respect as you would a face-to-face class environment.
  • Copy and paste discussion board posts into a text document on your personal computer before submitting them. Technical glitches in Canvas, while rare, do happen, and it is frustrating to lose hours of work.

Where do I go if I need help?

Being an online student can be challenging. Knowing where to turn when a question arises is important in mitigating the challenge. You have several resources available to you for help. The primary ones are:

  • The course materials
  • The instructor
  • Course FAQ
  • Hallway Conversations
  • ASU Help Desk
  • ASU Writing Centers

Questions about the course itself

If you have questions about how the course is run, a due date, grading criteria, things like that, the first place to look is the course materials, such as the syllabus and the assignment instructions. Make sure you have checked these before posing a question! If you cannot find your answer in the materials, look in Hallway Conversations. Perhaps someone else has asked the same question. If your question isn't already there (please check first!), then start a new thread in Hallway Conversations and ask it! You can also post in Hallway Conversations if you need clarification on a lesson, or if something you came across in the readings seems confusing. As long as the question is of a general nature, feel free to use the Hallway Conversations.

Questions about personal matters, such as grades

If you need to ask why you received a certain grade, or to negotiate a due date extension, or something of a personal nature like that, it is not appropriate to use the Q & A Forum, as that is a public space. Instead, you should email your instructor directly.

Questions about using Canvas, or help with resolving a technological problem

The instructor of the course may not have the expertise to assist you with technical glitches. For things like computer problems or Canvas failures, call or email the ASU Help Desk. The Help Desk can provide immediate technical assistance, and they are available 24 hours, 7 days a week, at 1-855-278-5080. They can also provide verification of technical problems, if you need to provide proof to your instructor. You can also visit https://my.asu.edu/service for online help articles and/or to submit an online help request.

Questions about writing format and grammar

Learning how to write in APA style can be challenging. Your instructor could help you with that, but it is more appropriate for you to seek help from the ASU Writing Centers, which are a free service for ASU students. The Writing Centers can also help you spot and correct grammar errors before you turn in your paper.
